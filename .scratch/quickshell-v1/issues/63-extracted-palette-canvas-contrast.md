## What to build

Palette extraction from workspace background images (`mapExtractedColorsToPalette`) produces tokens that read acceptably on the **shell** rim but often fail on the **canvas** — clock, welcome, quote, and widget copy sit directly on the background image with no surface card. Extracted `text` and `surface` colors can be too close to dominant image hues; the result feels muddy and not premium.

Improve extraction (and/or canvas widget styling) so extracted themes meet minimum contrast for canvas content while preserving shell glass readability. Consider: WCAG-ish contrast checks against a sampled background, separate canvas-surface token, subtle text shadow, or semi-opaque widget backing — pick the smallest fix that lands well on starter Work/Personal images.

## Acceptance criteria

- [x] Extracted palettes feel intentional (not raw dominant-pixel mapping) on at least the shipped starter images
- [x] Manual palette overrides in settings still win over extraction
- [x] Tests cover the contrast/mapping logic in `palette-extraction.ts`
